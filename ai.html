AI Technologies — Overview

Modern AI stacks combine data engineering, model training, deployment and observability. The trainings below are designed to be modular (half-day, full-day or multi-day) and hands-on.

Core topics covered

  • Foundational Models & LLMs: Overview of model families, pretraining vs fine-tuning, adapters, instruction-tuning, embeddings and cost/latency tradeoffs.
  • Generative AI & Prompt Engineering: Prompt templates, few-shot prompts, chain-of-thought, guardrails, evaluation and mitigation of hallucinations.
  • Vector Search & Retrieval: Building embeddings pipelines, vector stores (FAISS, Milvus, Chroma), semantic ranking and hybrid search strategies.
  • ML/Model Ops & Observability: Model CI/CD, performance monitoring, data drift detection, lineage and automated retraining strategies.
  • Responsible AI & Governance: Bias detection, explainability techniques, minimising data exposure and ensuring privacy/compliance.
  • Infrastructure & Cost: Serving patterns (serverless vs persistent), batching, caching and GPU/CPU tradeoffs for inference and training.

Sample Training Modules (detailed)

Intro to LLMs (Half-day)

Theory + labs: Model internals, tokenization, embeddings, and a hands-on lab calling an LLM, exploring prompts and evaluating outputs.

  • Lecture: Transformer basics
  • Lab: Prompt tuning and evaluation

Vector Search & Retrieval (Half-day)

Build an embeddings pipeline, index into a vector store, and prototype semantic search for a sample dataset.

  • Embedding models & techniques
  • Lab: Build and query a vector index

ML Ops & Observability (Full-day)

Design CI/CD for models, monitoring, drift detection and a sample retraining pipeline.

  • Model versioning & deployment
  • Lab: Create monitoring dashboards and alerts
