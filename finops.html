FinOps for AI — Why it matters

AI workloads behave differently from traditional cloud workloads. Model training, inference and storage of embeddings can cause unpredictable spend. FinOps for AI brings cost visibility, allocation, and governance while enabling teams to innovate.

Key practices and guidelines

  • Cost-aware architecture: Choose the right compute (GPUs/TPUs vs CPUs), use mixed-precision, model quantization, sharding, and consider managed inference services for predictable spend.
  • Experiment tracking & allocation: Tag experiments, track run-level costs, and allocate budgets to teams and projects to avoid cost leakage from exploratory workloads.
  • Budgets, alerts & forecasting: Build cost forecasts based on historical training runs, set automated alerts for spikes and define runbooks for cost incidents.
  • Efficiency & optimization: Use spot/preemptible instances, batch inference requests, enable caching and inference routing to cheaper models when latency allows.
  • Governance & policy: Define policies for model retention, artifact lifecycle, and who can launch large training jobs or provision GPU clusters.
